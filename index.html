What is Cloud Computing?

A delivery of on-demand computing resource

May 14, 2025 Pranit Potsure

Cloud computing is the delivery of computing services like servers, storage, databases, networking, software, and more over the internet. It allows individuals and businesses to access powerful computing resources on demand, without having to manage physical hardware.

Why Cloud Computing Matters:
Scalability: Instantly increase or decrease resources as needed.
Cost-Efficiency: Pay only for what you use.
Reliability: Cloud providers offer high uptime and backups.
Speed: Quickly deploy applications globally.

Core Cloud Service Models:
IaaS (Infrastructure as a Service): e.g., AWS EC2
PaaS (Platform as a Service): e.g., Heroku
SaaS (Software as a Service): e.g., Gmail

Popular Cloud Providers

May 25, 2025 Pranit Potsure

AWS: Most mature cloud platform. Services: EC2, S3, RDS, Lambda.

Azure: Strong in enterprise and hybrid cloud. Services: Azure VM, AD, DevOps.

GCP: Best for analytics and AI. Services: BigQuery, Vertex AI, GKE.

IBM Cloud: Known for Watson AI and hybrid enterprise solutions.

Oracle Cloud: Ideal for Oracle DB and enterprise workloads.

Alibaba Cloud: Popular in Asia. Strong in big data and scalable infrastructure.
